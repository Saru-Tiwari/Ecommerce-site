Caring Tips for Succulents(plants)

To take care of your houseplants, you should water them regularly, but not too much. Overwatering can damage the roots and prevent the plant from growing. Poke your finger into the soil up to your knuckle to check if the soil is dry or moist before watering. The frequency and amount of watering may vary depending on the type of plant, the size of the pot, and the season. Plants with lush, thick leaves require more water than plants with waxy or leathery leaves. Use good quality soil and a balanced, 10-10-10 fertilizer for most houseplants. Repot them when necessary. Ensure adequate light for your plants. Keep them near a window or under artificial light. To prevent dust from accumulating on the leaves, you can shower them with room-temperature water or wipe them with a cloth.
